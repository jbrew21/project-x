# LinkedIn Post — Jack Brewster

---

I spent 3 years at NewsGuard tracking how misinformation spreads online.

I broke the story that traced the "Haitian migrants eating pets" hoax to its source. I ran the study that showed TikTok's algorithm feeds misinfo to teenagers within 40 minutes.

After all that research, one pattern kept showing up:

The content isn't the problem. The framing is.

A kid says something cute at a space launch — that's a wholesome moment. But add "Kid just SMOKED a CNN reporter" and suddenly it's a culture war. Same content. Completely different emotional reaction. That's ragebait. And it's everywhere.

Oxford named "rage bait" the Word of the Year in 2025. Research in Science found false content spreads 70% faster than truth. X literally pays people per engagement — some creators earn six figures from your angry comments alone.

So I built something.

Today Newsreel is launching Ragebait Tracker (@ragetrack on X) — an AI bot that anyone can tag on any tweet to get an instant analysis of whether you're being manipulated.

It rates tweets 1-10, explains the exact tactic being used, and exposes the gap between what the tweet makes you FEEL and what actually happened.

It's politically neutral. It tracks accounts on the left and the right. It doesn't care about ideology — it cares about manipulation tactics.

Try it yourself: go to any tweet on X, reply with "@ragetrack is this ragebait?" and see what happens.

We also publish a daily thread rating the 5 worst ragebait tweets from the last 24 hours. Think of it as a media literacy service with a sense of humor.

This is what Newsreel is about — building AI tools that help people navigate information instead of being manipulated by it.

If you're a journalist, researcher, educator, or just someone who's tired of being the crop in someone else's engagement farm — give it a try and let me know what you think.
