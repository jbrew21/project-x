# Newsreel AI Launches "Ragebait Tracker" — An AI Bot That Rates and Roasts Manipulative Tweets in Real Time

**The tool from the journalist who broke the Springfield "eating pets" story uses AI to expose outrage-farming tactics on X**

**Brooklyn, NY — April 2, 2026** — Newsreel, the AI-powered news platform built to combat misinformation, today launched Ragebait Tracker (@ragetrack on X) — an automated AI bot that analyzes tweets for manipulation tactics and rates them on a 1-10 "ragebait scale" with sharp, funny breakdowns of exactly how you're being played.

Anyone on X can reply to any tweet, tag @ragetrack, and get an instant AI-powered analysis explaining whether — and how — the tweet is engineered to make you angry for engagement.

"I spent years at NewsGuard and Forbes tracking how misinformation spreads. The pattern is almost always the same: take something real, add inflammatory framing, and watch both sides engage," said Jack Brewster, founder and CEO of Newsreel. "Ragebait Tracker automates what I used to do manually — it strips away the framing and shows you what's actually there."

## How It Works

Ragebait Tracker uses a proprietary AI analysis system trained on peer-reviewed research into outrage mechanics, algorithmic amplification, and engagement farming tactics. When tagged on a tweet, it runs the content through a signal-based scoring rubric that checks for:

- **Inflammatory framing** — WWE-style language ("SMOKED", "DESTROYED", "OWNED") applied to mundane events
- **Culture war injection** — political framing forced onto apolitical moments
- **False suppression narratives** — "the media won't cover this" claims about widely-covered events
- **Emotional manipulation** — emoji priming, censored swear words, and rhetorical traps
- **Context stripping** — key information removed to maximize outrage

The bot then posts a rating with a punchy explanation exposing the gap between the tweet's framing and reality.

## Why Now

Oxford University Press named "rage bait" its Word of the Year for 2025, reflecting a threefold increase in usage. Research published in *Science* found that false content is 70% more likely to be retweeted than truth, while each moral-emotional word in a tweet increases its spread by approximately 20%.

Meanwhile, X's creator monetization program has turned outrage into a paycheck — some engagement farmers earn over $150,000 annually, primarily from hate comments and angry shares.

"The incentive structure is broken," said Brewster. "Platforms pay people to make you angry. Ragebait Tracker is a public service — it makes the manipulation visible so people can make informed decisions about what they share."

## The Newsreel Mission

Ragebait Tracker is the latest tool from Newsreel, which Brewster founded after spending years on the front lines of the misinformation crisis. At NewsGuard, he broke the story tracing the viral "Haitian migrants eating pets" claim to its source and conducted a landmark 2022 study revealing how TikTok's algorithm systematically feeds misinformation to young users. Previously, he covered politics, misinformation, and extremism for Forbes, Time, and Vice.

Newsreel's core product is an interactive news app designed to make quality journalism accessible to younger audiences. Ragebait Tracker extends that mission to X, where the need for media literacy tools is arguably greatest.

"We're not trying to tell people what to think," said Brewster. "We're trying to show people when someone is trying to manipulate what they feel. There's a difference."

## Daily Ragebait Report

In addition to on-demand analysis, Ragebait Tracker publishes a daily thread highlighting the most egregious ragebait tweets from the previous 24 hours — scanning high-profile accounts across the political spectrum and rating the worst offenders.

The bot monitors accounts on both the left and right, including aggregator pages, political commentators, and known engagement farmers. Its analysis is politically neutral — the scoring rubric evaluates framing tactics, not ideology.
